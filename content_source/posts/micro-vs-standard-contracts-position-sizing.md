A micro lot is 1/100th the size of a standard lot. In forex, that means each pip is worth $0.10 instead of $10. This difference determines whether a given account size can trade with proper risk management — or is forced to oversize from the start.

---

## Lot Size Hierarchy: Forex

| Lot Type | Size (units) | Pip Value (EUR/USD) | Min Account (1% risk, 50-pip stop) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano (0.001) | 100 units | $0.01/pip | $50 |
| Micro (0.01) | 1,000 units | $0.10/pip | $500 |
| Mini (0.10) | 10,000 units | $1.00/pip | $5,000 |
| Standard (1.00) | 100,000 units | $10.00/pip | $50,000 |
| 2 Standard (2.00) | 200,000 units | $20.00/pip | $100,000 |

The minimum account column shows the balance needed to risk exactly 1% per trade with a 50-pip stop. A trader with $10,000 using 1 standard lot at a 50-pip stop risks $500 — 5% of the account, 5× the intended risk.

## When to Use Micro Lots (Forex)

**Micro lots (0.01) are appropriate when:**

1. **Account balance is under $5,000** — this is the primary use case. A $2,000 account at 1% risk ($20) with a 40-pip stop: Lot Size = $20 ÷ (40 × $10) = 0.05 lots (5 micro lots)

2. **Learning to trade** — real money but small enough that losses don't cause financial stress

3. **Testing a new strategy** — minimum viable real-money test without significant exposure

4. **Funded account demo preparation** — practice the sizing discipline at scaled-down dollar amounts

**Micro lots are NOT appropriate when:**
- Your account is above $10,000 and micro lots produce dollar P&L that's psychologically irrelevant
- You need to run 50+ micro positions to achieve 1% risk (impractical order management)

---

## When to Use Standard Lots (Forex)

**Standard lots (1.00) are appropriate when:**

1. **Account balance is above $50,000** — at 1% risk ($500) with a 50-pip stop: 1 standard lot exactly

2. **Funded account at $50,000+** — funded traders at this level use standard lots as their primary unit

3. **Institutional or semi-professional trading** — where large dollar P&L per pip is required

**Standard lots are NOT appropriate for:**
- Accounts under $25,000 (most stops produce excessive risk at 1 lot)
- Learning traders — the dollar swings at full lot sizing create emotional decision-making

---

## The In-Between: Mini Lots (0.10)

Mini lots (0.10 = 10,000 units, $1/pip) are the most practical size for accounts between $5,000 and $25,000. They provide:
- Meaningful dollar P&L ($1–$10 per pip depending on stop)
- Sufficient precision for 1% risk sizing
- Lower psychological pressure than standard lots

**$10,000 account, 1% risk ($100), 50-pip stop:**
```
Lot Size = $100 ÷ (50 × $10) = 0.20 lots = 2 mini lots
```

---

## Precision Sizing: Mixing Lot Sizes

The cleanest risk targeting uses a mix of lot sizes. For example, a $15,000 account at 1% risk ($150) with a 40-pip stop:

```
Lot Size = $150 ÷ (40 × $10) = 0.375 lots
```

Exact 0.375 rounds to 0.38 (if broker supports it) or:
- 3 mini lots (0.30) = $120 risk (0.8% — acceptable)
- 3 mini + 7 micro lots (0.37) = $148 risk (0.99% — very close)

Most brokers support 0.01 lot increments, allowing near-perfect targeting.

## Moving from Micro to Standard: The Progression

| Account Stage | Lot Type | Typical Position | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| $500–$2,000 | Micro (0.01–0.05) | 0.02–0.05 lots | Only option at 1% risk |
| $2,000–$10,000 | Micro to Mini | 0.05–0.20 lots | Growing precision |
| $10,000–$30,000 | Mini (0.10–0.50) | 0.10–0.60 lots | Full mini range |
| $30,000–$100,000 | Mini to Standard | 0.60–2.00 lots | Transitioning to standard |
| $100,000+ | Standard (1.00+) | 1.00–4.00 lots | Full institutional sizing |

**When to upgrade**: when the required lot size consistently exceeds 10 micro lots AND your account is above $5,000. Micro lots above 10–15 count become unwieldy and many brokers charge extra spreads.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I trade micro or standard lots as a beginner?**
Micro lots. At any account below $5,000, standard lots produce excessive risk percentages. Start with micro and progress as your balance grows.

**Are micro lots good for funded trader practice?**
Yes — trading micro lots at 10× reduced dollar amounts lets you practice funded-account-style discipline (0.5% risk rules, daily caps) without the evaluation pressure.

**What is the difference between 0.01 and 1.00 lot?**
0.01 is a micro lot (1,000 units, $0.10/pip). 1.00 is a standard lot (100,000 units, $10/pip). Standard is 100× larger than micro in every dimension.

**When should I move from micro to standard lots?**
When your account consistently requires 10+ micro lots per trade (meaning the account is large enough for mini or standard lots), and when you've demonstrated consistent risk discipline over 3+ months at the smaller size.

**How do I get precise lot sizes between increments?**
Use 0.01 lot increments (most brokers support this). A target of 0.37 lots = 3 mini lots (0.30) + 7 micro lots (0.07). Add them as a single order if your broker supports fractional lot entry.
